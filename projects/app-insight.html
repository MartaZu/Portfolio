Executive KPI Dashboard

Challenge

Data overload at the top

A2A's top management were drowning in spreadsheets and siloed reporting tools. Strategic KPIs lived in different systems, updated at different cadences, with no shared view of what mattered most. The challenge: design a single application that gives executives a clear, real-time picture of the group's performance — without adding another dashboard that nobody opens after week one.

Approach

Designing for the five-minute window

I interviewed 12 executives across business units to map their actual information-seeking behaviours — when they checked data, on which device, for what decisions. The insight: most strategic check-ins happen in transit, in meetings, or at the start and end of the day. This shaped everything: mobile-first layout, scannable hierarchy, and — most unusually — an audio mode that reads key KPI summaries aloud as a personal podcast brief, designed for the commute.

Solution

Three layers: see, discuss, listen

App Insight is structured around three complementary modes. The dashboard layer gives a scannable KPI overview with self-BI controls — executives can filter, drill down, and customise their view without IT support. The social layer enables inline comments and reactions directly on data points, turning solo number-reading into a shared conversation. The audio layer generates a personalised daily brief read aloud — a differentiating feature that emerged directly from user research and had no precedent in the existing toolset.

I owned both the design and the functional definition across the full 12-month development cycle, working directly with engineering to manage scope and ensure fidelity between prototype and production.
