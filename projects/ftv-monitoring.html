Challenge

Making the invisible visible

A2A manages a growing portfolio of photovoltaic plants across Italy, but monitoring them relied on fragmented tools and manual reporting. Field operators lacked real-time visibility; management had no single source of truth. The challenge: design a platform that could serve both an operational audience (technicians in the field) and a strategic one (management monitoring commercial performance), while also being robust enough to sell to external plant operators as a standalone product.

Approach

Dual-track research, single platform

I ran parallel discovery tracks — shadowing field operators during plant inspections and conducting structured interviews with management. This revealed two fundamentally different mental models of "monitoring": for operators, it meant real-time alerts and fault tracking; for executives, it meant trend lines and ROI. The design challenge was unifying these without compromising either experience. I facilitated co-design workshops with both groups to validate assumptions before any screen was drawn.

Solution

One platform, two entry points

FTV Monitoring is structured around a role-aware dashboard that adapts its primary view based on the user's context. Operators land on a live plant map with real-time fault indicators and a prioritised action queue. Managers land on an aggregated performance view with production trend lines, energy yield, and commercial KPIs. A shared alert system keeps both groups informed without noise.

I designed a modular widget system that allows plants of different scales and configurations to be onboarded without custom development — a key requirement for external commercialisation. The design system was built in Figma and handed off with a full token library to reduce developer interpretation time.
