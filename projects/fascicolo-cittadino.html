Fascicolo del Cittadino — app concept

Challenge

Public services, human scale

Milan's citizens interact with dozens of municipal services — permits, health records, tax declarations, school enrolments — through fragmented channels with inconsistent digital experiences. The Fascicolo del Cittadino was conceived as a single, unified app: a citizen's personal file that aggregates their relationship with the city. The challenge: design something genuinely usable by the full spectrum of Milan's population, from digital natives to elderly residents with low tech confidence.

Approach

Citizens as co-designers

The project followed a full human-centred design process over six months. I led the research and benchmarking phase, then designed and facilitated co-design workshops with citizen panels — deliberately including participants from underrepresented groups. This early involvement meant that by the time screens were being built, the core navigation logic and key service flows had already been tested with real users — dramatically reducing late-stage rework.

Solution

One app, every citizen

The Fascicolo del Cittadino is organised around life events rather than administrative categories — moving home, having a child, paying fines — reflecting how citizens actually think about their relationship with the municipality, not how the back-office is structured. A persistent personal profile surfaces the citizen's active requests, upcoming deadlines, and documents in one place.

The design system I built prioritised accessibility: WCAG AA contrast compliance throughout, large touch targets, plain-language microcopy, and a consistent pattern library that the development team could implement reliably. Usability testing with five participant profiles validated the core flows before final handoff.
