Cassa Centrale Banca — app redesign

Challenge

An app that wasn't working — but why?

Cassa Centrale Banca's mobile banking app was underperforming: low engagement, below-average store ratings, and a growing gap between the bank's digital ambitions and what customers actually experienced. The challenge: before investing in a redesign, understand precisely why — from both the management perspective and the end-user perspective.

Approach

Evidence-first, opinion-second

I led a rigorous four-month research phase combining qualitative depth with quantitative breadth. Senior management interviews captured strategic intent and internal tensions. In-app feedback and store reviews surfaced the actual pain points users expressed in their own words. Competitive benchmarking situated the product against best-in-class peers and adjacent comparables — framing not just where the app fell short, but what a credible step forward would look like.

Solution

A research foundation that drove real change

The output of the research phase was a synthesis framework that mapped user pain points to business priorities, identified quick wins versus structural issues, and gave the design team an evidence-backed brief for the redesign. Key findings: users struggled most with navigation hierarchy and transaction legibility; management had underestimated the importance of the push notification experience; competitor analysis revealed an emerging pattern in personalised financial insights that the app was entirely missing.

The app was redesigned on the basis of these insights. Post-release store ratings improved, validating the research direction.
